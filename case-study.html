Understanding the Users

Primary Persona: The Beginner User

Workout Attitude:

  • 6 weeks into her gym membership ‚Üí unsure what to do
  • wants & needs guidance, but finds asking for help scary,
  • finds cardio machines a bit bland,
  • finds the traditional weight machines & free weights (& the people who use them) intimidating

Why she matters:

  • Beginners represent an estimated 60-70% of the Smart Strength machine user base
  • Highest churn risk occurs in the first months
  • Successful beginners become loyal intermediate users who train 3-4x/week and drive referrals
  • The difference between retention and churn often comes down to whether users feel competent and see progress

  • Why she chose EGYM:
    • No decision paralysis about weight selection‚Äîthe machine recommends it
    • Automatic setup saves her from fumbling with seat adjustments and pin placements
    • Real-time visual guidance (the curve) gives her something to follow
    • Less intimidating than the free weights area

  • Where the experience breaks down:

    She can't interpret the success screen metrics. Is 44% training quality "acceptable for a beginner" or "you're doing something wrong"? She has no frame of reference. Without understanding whether she's improving or what to adjust, she's less likely to return after the initial motivation fades.

  • What would actually help:

    Clear, specific feedback that says: "You're rushing the lowering phase. Count to 3 on the way down. If that's too difficult, reduce the weight by 2kg."

Secondary Persona: The Intermediate User

Workout Attitude:

  • has been training consistently for 8 months
  • tech-savvy data enthusiast
  • a bit competitive ‚Üí wants to know how he compares to others
  • wants to improve his form
  • wants to experiment to challenge himself
  • doesn‚Äôt want the faff of having to adjust the traditional machines and have to remember what his previous weights were

Why he matters:

  • Intermediates are lower churn risk but drive word-of-mouth growth
  • They provide valuable feedback on data accuracy and feature requests
  • They eventually become the "regulars" who create gym community
  • Losing them signals product isn't growing with users

  • Why he chose EGYM:
    • Automatic tracking eliminates manual logging burden
    • Negative training mode enables eccentric overload not possible with traditional equipment
    • Objective performance metrics feed his analytical mindset
    • Time efficiency‚Äîno equipment adjustment between sets

  • Current frustrations:
    • Success screen doesn't show enough detail‚Äîhe wants phase-specific breakdowns
    • Can't easily see progress over time without switching to the app
    • No way to compare his performance against others or know if he's "good" for his level
    • Program recommendations feel one-size-fits-all
  • What he needs:
    Access to the granular data EGYM already captures. He wants to see "87% concentric, 41% eccentric"‚Äîthat level of detail is valuable to him, not overwhelming. He'd also appreciate peer comparison (genuinely curious where he ranks) and historical trend data.

Measurement Plan
  • Quantitative
    • Automatic (Machine Telemetry):
      • User sessions (who, when, which machine, duration)
      • Success screen interactions (time spent, sections expanded, clicks)
      • Performance data (eccentric/concentric phase quality per set)
      • System performance (load times, errors)
    • Weekly Snapshots:
      • Cohort retention rates (beta vs. control)
      • Engagement metrics (time on screen, interaction rates)
      • Performance trends (form quality improvements)
    • Monthly Analysis:
      • Cohort survival curves (visualize retention over time)
      • Segmentation (does retention differ by age, gender, machine type?)

  • Qualitative
    • Micro-surveys (10% random sample):
      • Trigger: After workout completion
      • Question: "Did today's success screen help you understand what to improve?" (Yes / Somewhat / No)
      • Follow-up (if No): "What was confusing?" (Open text, optional)
    • User Interviews (5-10 users/month from beta gyms):
      • Week 2-3 of using new success screen
      • Questions: What do you remember from success screen? Did you change anything based on feedback? How does it make you feel?
      • Goal: Understand why retention improves (or doesn't)
    • Gym Staff Check-ins (Bi-weekly during beta):
      • Brief calls with trainers/front desk staff
      • Questions: Are members asking about the new screen? Any complaints? Any praise?
      • Goal: Early warning system for issues

    • Daily:
      • Real-time dashboard of engagement metrics (time on screen, interaction rates)

      • Beta steering meeting

      • Executive summary

Appendix

Tertiary Persona: The Trainer / Gym Staff

Current workflow:

  • Introduces new members to machines
  • Monitors form during sessions
  • Answers recurring questions: "Is this weight right for me?" "Am I doing this correctly?"
  • Tracks member progress manually or via EGYM trainer app

Why he matters:

  • Trainers influence member adoption‚Äîif they're skeptical, members won't engage
  • They're boots-on-the-ground feedback source for what actually helps users
  • Their satisfaction impacts gym owner satisfaction with EGYM platform
  • They're potential power users of advanced features

  • Pain points:
    • Spends significant time on basic questions that could be automated
    • Hard to scale‚Äîcan only watch one person at a time
    • Members train independently and develop bad habits between sessions
    • No easy way to identify which members need intervention

  • What he needs to become an advocate:
    • Trainer dashboard showing which members have poor form scores (enables proactive outreach)
    • Ability to customize coaching tips per member (maintains his expert role)
    • Credit for improvements‚Äî"Your form improved because of Tom's coaching and machine feedback"
    • Evidence that automated coaching improves member outcomes (makes his job easier, not obsolete)

Design Principles
  • Tablet-optimized: Fixed-height panels with accordion behavior prevent overwhelm on smaller screens.
  • Progressive disclosure: Most important information (coaching + execution quality) visible by default. Comparisons hidden until requested.
  • Beginner-first language: Terms like "Muskelanspannung" and "Muskelentspannung" with tooltips that explain concentric/eccentric phases in accessible language.
  • Consistent architecture: Same insights available across machine UI, member app, and trainer app for a cohesive experience.

    • Achievement badges for consistent training
